Badge triggers: · filing = the company's fundamentals changed between snapshots (a new 10-K/10-Q arrived) — an information-driven change; · price = identical fundamentals, only the price crossed a scoring threshold — likely noise, prone to flickering back.

Trade policy: All changes holds exactly the qualifying list each period. New filings only trades a company only when new fundamentals arrive — a price-driven qualifier is bought once its next filing confirms it, and a price-driven dropout is held until a filing confirms the exit. Price moves only is the mirror image: it acts only on changes not backed by new information.

Simulation assumes equal-weight buys at each rebalance close, no trading costs or taxes. Holdings without a newer price are carried flat. Survivorship and split effects may skew older periods — treat this as a rough proof of concept, not investment advice.
